Past Papers Guide — How to Use Them Effectively

6 min read

Past papers are the single most powerful tool in MDCAT preparation. Solving them correctly can boost your score by 20–30 marks.

When to Start Solving Past Papers

Start solving past papers after completing at least 60% of your syllabus. Don't wait until you've finished everything — early exposure helps you understand the exam pattern.

How to Solve Effectively

  • Solve under timed conditions — 3.5 hours for 200 MCQs
  • Don't check answers mid-paper
  • After finishing, analyse every wrong answer
  • Note down repeated topics and weak areas

Pattern Recognition

Certain topics repeat every year. Biology chapters like Genetics, Cell Biology and Reproduction are consistently tested. In Chemistry, Organic reactions and Stoichiometry appear frequently.

How Many Papers to Solve

Aim to solve at least 5–6 past papers before your exam. The last 3 years (2022–2024) are most relevant as they reflect the current PMC pattern.

Top 10 Mistakes to Avoid in MDCAT

5 min read

These are the most common mistakes that cost students marks in MDCAT — avoid them to maximise your score.

1. Not Following a Study Plan

Studying randomly without a schedule leads to incomplete coverage. Always follow a structured timetable.

2. Skipping English

Many students ignore English thinking it's easy. 8% weightage means 16 marks — don't leave them on the table.

3. Not Solving Past Papers

Past papers reveal the exam pattern. Students who skip them are unprepared for the actual question style.

4. Memorising Without Understanding

MDCAT tests application, not just memory. Understand concepts, especially in Biology and Chemistry.

5. Poor Time Management in Exam

Spending too long on one question is a common trap. Move on and come back later.

6. Not Revising Regularly

Studying a chapter once is not enough. Revise every chapter at least 3 times before the exam.

7. Ignoring Weak Areas

Students tend to study what they already know. Focus extra time on weak chapters.

8. Studying Without MCQ Practice

Reading notes is not enough. Practice MCQs after every chapter to test your understanding.

9. Panic on Exam Day

Stay calm. If you've prepared well, trust your preparation. Anxiety causes careless mistakes.

10. Changing Correct Answers

Research shows first instinct is usually correct. Don't change answers unless you're 100% sure.
